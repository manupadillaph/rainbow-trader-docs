    2.3 The Friction Mechanism (Data-Driven)

    The mechanism is:

    1. A model with better S2/F1 detects more patterns → generates more trade signals
    2. More signals → more trades (the gate lets more through)

    3. Each trade costs friction (slippage + commission round-trip)

    4. Profitable trials average 106 trades

    5. Losing trials average 230 trades
    6. Trade difference: 123 extra trades
    7. Average friction per trade: $0.92
    8. Friction drag from extra trades: $114

    When PnL is normalized per trade (removing the trade-count effect), positive correlations emerge — confirming that better models DO capture slightly more edge per trade, but not enough to offset the friction from trading more often.

    Friction Paradox Insight: The global S2-vs-Alpha-RC correlation is r=+0.045, while Trades-vs-Alpha-RC is r=-0.802 and Trades-vs-PnL is r=-0.803. This is the core friction paradox: signal quality does not automatically monetize because more signal often means more trades and more friction. Implication for rescue: models must be MORE SELECTIVE (higher threshold, stricter gate) to reduce trade count without destroying regime-relative edge.

    12. Key Takeaways for Tier B Rescue Design

    Data-driven synthesis from 1747 trials (≥5 trades) out of 1750 total completed:

    The Friction Paradox (CRITICAL)

    1. S2 vs Alpha RC: r=+0.045 — Thesis skill is only partially explained by signal quality
    2. Trades vs Alpha RC: r=-0.802 — More trades usually erode regime-relative edge
    3. S2 vs PnL: r=+0.037 — Signal quality does NOT cleanly predict monetization
    4. Trades vs PnL: r=-0.803 — More trades STRONGLY predicts losses
    5. Alpha-RC-positive trials average 116 trades — selectivity still matters on the thesis lens
    6. Only 0.8% of trials have positive Alpha RC, and 0.8% have positive PnL
    7. Profitable trials average 106 trades vs 230 for losing (Δ=123 trades)

    Overfitting Dynamics

    1. Feature count vs S1-S2 gap: r=+0.285 — More features = more overfitting risk
    2. 45 forced features require aggressive regularization to prevent memorization
    3. Weight_decay shows surprising positive correlation with gap (r=+0.207)

    Rescue Configuration Recommendations

    1. Test selectivity levers explicitly: higher prediction thresholds and entry-gate neighborhoods
    2. Entry gate weight vs Trades: r=+0.011, vs PnL: r=-0.023
    3. Keep regularization model-specific: search broader dropout/weight_decay, but avoid one blanket bias
    4. Increase epoch budget selectively: favor families or runs that actually hit the budget ceiling
    5. Seed diversity: Use top-N from multiple metrics (Obj, S2, S3, Alpha RC, PnL, Sharpe) to avoid converging on one metric that doesn't predict the whole picture
