    Proportionality Lens

    This section asks whether the rerun improved only because it traded less, or whether it also improved the thesis-primary RC skill lens and kept cleaner per-trade economics.

    What To Look At

    What this section is: a fast reading guide for deciding whether a rerun looks genuinely promising.

    • First check Interp Alpha RC: Better than proportional is the thesis-strongest signal. It means Alpha RC improved more than a simple reduction in turnover would suggest.
    • Then check Dual Lens: YES means the rerun is better than proportional in both PnL and Alpha RC.
    • Then check Interp PnL: it tells you whether the economic outcome also improved beyond the raw turnover cut.
    • Then check Gross Chg%: positive values are especially important because they suggest the rerun kept or improved gross edge, not just reduced friction.
    • Then compare Orig Gross/trade versus New Gross/trade: if the new value is higher, the bot appears to be selecting better opportunities per trade.
    • PnL Imp% and Alpha RC Imp% are both useful, but by themselves they can be inflated by merely trading less. They matter most when they are paired with positive or less-negative Gross Chg%.
    • A very high Trade Red% with poor Gross Chg% usually means the threshold became too strict: the bot traded much less, but also discarded too much gross edge.
